"No, not now, later!" he said.

"Very well, later, but you must certainly tell me. I’m not afraid of anything. I want to know everything. Now it is settled."

He added: "Settled that you’ll take me whatever I may be—you won’t give me up? Yes?"

"Yes, yes."

Their conversation was interrupted by Mademoiselle Linon, who with an affected but tender smile came to congratulate her favorite pupil. Before she had gone, the servants came in with their congratulations. Then relations arrived, and there began that state of blissful absurdity from which Levin did not emerge till the day after his wedding. Levin was in a continual state of awkwardness and discomfort, but the intensity of his happiness went on all the while increasing. He felt continually that a great deal was being expected of him—what, he did not know;
