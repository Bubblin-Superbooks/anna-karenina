"So I heard. May I see the letter?"

"I’ll get it directly. But he doesn’t refuse; on the contrary, Stiva has hopes," said Dolly, stopping in the doorway.

"I haven’t, and indeed I don’t wish it," said Anna.

"What’s this? Does Kitty consider it degrading to meet me?" thought Anna when she was alone. "Perhaps she’s right, too. But it’s not for her, the girl who was in love with Vronsky, it’s not for her to show me that, even if it is true. I know that in my position I can’t be received by any decent woman. I knew that from the first moment I sacrificed everything to him. And this is my reward! Oh, how I hate him! And what did I come here for? I’m worse here, more miserable." She heard from the next room the sisters’ voices in consultation.
